Where I can help

Practical cleanup for the IT workflows teams rely on every week.

The goal is not an enterprise transformation project. It is focused, useful help for the operational places where growing teams start to feel friction, lose context, or depend on one person remembering every step.

Onboarding, offboarding, and access cleanup

  • Onboarding and offboarding that is too manual, inconsistent, or dependent on tribal knowledge
  • Access requests scattered across Slack, Jira, email, spreadsheets, and people's heads
  • Identity and access workflows that need cleanup, clearer ownership, or better handoffs
  • Google Workspace, Okta, Slack, Jira/JSM, Jamf, and SaaS admin process review

Operations, documentation, and lightweight systems

  • SaaS tools that do not connect cleanly or have unclear ownership
  • Documentation, request workflows, and repeatable IT process design
  • Lightweight automation opportunities that reduce manual work without overcomplicating things
  • Stability support when IT headcount has been reduced or the team is stretched thin

Common signs

You might need this if IT work is starting to depend on memory.

New hires rely on too many manual steps to get access
Offboarding depends on memory or scattered checklists
HR, IT, Finance, and managers are not aligned on employee lifecycle steps
Access requests live across Slack, Jira, email, and informal DMs
SaaS access ownership is unclear
IT headcount changed and the remaining team needs steadier systems
Documentation exists but is outdated or hard to use
The team is growing but IT processes still feel informal
