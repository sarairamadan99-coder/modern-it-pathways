Ticket Intake

Ticket intake is simply how people ask IT for help. A clear intake path helps employees know where to go and helps IT keep track of what needs attention.

Why it matters

Clear intake keeps work from scattering everywhere.

When there is no clear path, requests show up in DMs, hallway conversations, Slack threads, and email. IT loses track of work, employees do not know where to go, and urgent requests get mixed together with questions that can wait.

Starting options

Pick the simplest path your team will actually use.

1. Dedicated IT email inbox

Easiest to start and good for small teams. It can become hard to track as volume grows.

2. Slack or Teams channel

Approachable and visible for quick questions. Requests can get buried if nobody owns review.

3. Lightweight help desk tool

Better for tracking ownership, status, history, and repeated issues over time.

4. Jira Service Management

Useful if the company already uses Jira. Start simple, even with the free edition, and avoid overbuilding the portal.

Practical recommendation

Start simple, then improve as patterns become clear.

Start with the simplest intake method your team will actually use. Once you can see what people ask for, what repeats, and where requests get stuck, it becomes much easier to choose the next improvement.

A lightweight process that people follow beats a perfect process that everyone avoids.

Checklist

A few questions to test your intake path.

  • Do people know where to ask for help?
  • Does IT have one place to review requests?
  • Can you tell what is open, waiting, or resolved?
  • Can you spot repeated issues over time?
